All about Nuts!!!

Nuts are sources of protein, fats, fibres, vitamins, and minerals. They also regulate body weight as their fats are not fully absorbed. Nuts regulate food intake, and help burn energy.

Here are our Lovely Nuts!!

The almond is often thought of as a nut, though it's technically a drupe fruit produced by the almond tree (Prunus dulcis; Prunus amygdalus). It's native to North Africa, West Asia, and the Mediterranean, though most almonds today are grown in the United States, Spain, and Italy. Eat almonds where ever you found them!!

The peanut, also known as the groundnut, goober, pindar or monkey nut, and taxonomically classified as Arachis hypogaea, is a legume crop grown mainly for its edible seeds. It is widely grown in the tropics and subtropics, being important to both small and large commercial producers.(Wikipedia)

The cashew tree (Anacardium occidentale) is a tropical evergreen tree that produces the cashew seed and the cashew apple accessory fruit. The tree can grow as high as 14 m (46 ft), but the dwarf cultivars, growing up to 6 m (20 ft), prove more profitable, with earlier maturity and greater yields. (Wikipedia)

The Brazil nut (Bertholletia excelsa) is a South American tree in the family Lecythidaceae, and it is also the name of the tree's commercially harvested edible seeds. It is one of the largest and longest-lived trees in the Amazon rainforest. The fruit and its nutshell – containing the edible Brazil nut – are relatively large.

Nuts and seeds are good sources of protein, healthy fats, fibres, vitamins, and minerals. Nuts and seeds regulate body weight as their fats are not fully absorbed, they regulate food intake, and help burn energy.
